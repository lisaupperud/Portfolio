ABOUT ME

Work History

I got my first job at 15 as a newspaper carrier. At 16, I moved onto baby-sitting for a couple of years before I got my first job at a café at 18, and within a few months I got another job at a restaurant as a waitress. - From there on I worked as both a waitress and later on, bartender, for different restaurants and nightclubs around Stockholm.

I found the restaurant business intriguing, there was a lot of room for personal development and growth within my job roles. Dealing with all the different guests and their personalities, learning to work with people who's style differs from your own, working under pressure from different directions - the kitchen, the guests, the boss etc.

Working in the restaurant industry has helped me develope strong communication skills, because you often work with or for people who differ from yourself meaning you have to be clear in your communication and make sure you understand eachother correctly. My biggest take-away? Ask every question you have to make sure both parts understand eachother.

The job requires a high level of resilience under pressure and the ability to stay focused under pressure, meaning you have to be able to stay calm and strategic even when a lot is asked from you. To deal with this, I make sure to be prepared for everything that might be asked of me - even if I know it's going to be a calm evening, I prep for an evening of chaos. If my predictions end up true, I just did some extra work - but when that group of 20 people drop in, I'm prepared. This has also made me realize that sometimes you can't live up to every expectation of you, but as long as you do your absolute best, you can be proud of yourself.

My biggest lesson from my years in the service industry? Practice makes perfect. Looking back on my first years as a waitress and bartender, I can't help but smile at how little I knew—and how much I've learned just by showing up every day and doing the work, even on the nights that felt like torture. That's how I built the confidence to feel secure and capable in my role. I'm now looking forward to the day when I can reflect on my journey as a developer in the same way—smiling at how far I've come through years of hands-on experience, problem-solving, and quick thinking under pressure.

Programming

Go to Projects

I had my first encounter with code in 2022, when my brother showed me a frontend project he was working on in school: a virtual library. It looked really cool, and I was very impressed that he had managed to build such a thing. He was studying to become a Fullstack Java Developer, just as I am now. He showed me some of the code behind the application, and tried to explain to me what it did, but to me it just looked like random English words in different colors.

At the time, I was taking a few courses through Adult Education to gain my Upper Secondary Education diploma. I had a few more courses to complete, and in 2024 I decided to try a 5-week course in C# and .NET. I found the course to be quite tough the first couple of weeks — it felt like I was trying to learn a new language AND the whole science behind computers. But I found it really fun. I love the feeling when something finally 'clicks'. I remember when I was going to try to run some code for the first time ("Hello World!") and I couldn’t get it to work for 20 minutes. Why? I forgot the semicolon at the end of the WriteLine!

I started seeing everyday life with new eyes, suddenly having an understanding of how the technology around us works. Seeing how much we rely on working code to make our everyday lives run smoothly — from the alarm on our phones that wakes us up every morning, to the app where we can check if our train to work is on time, to the registers in the grocery store where we buy our lunch. It was something I had never reflected on before, and with that realization, I became in awe of the world of code — and I wanted to be a part of that, and a part of developing code for the future.

So I applied to a Fullstack Java Developer at Stockholms Tekniska Institut (STI). I was really excited when I got my admission letter, as I was looking forward to learning about this new world that I had just dipped my toe into. I also love learning, about anything really. Anything that challenges me intellectually is intriguing to me, as I feel like challenges are the biggest thing for personal development and growth. I've learned so much this past year in school, and I love that things that didn't make sense a few months ago are now clear as day to me.
